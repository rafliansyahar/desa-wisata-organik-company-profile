History of Lombok Kulon Organic Tourism Village

Lombok Kulon Village is located in Wonosari District, Bondowoso Regency, East Java. Since 2009, Lombok Kulon village has adopted the concept of “Organic Village”, meaning that almost all agricultural products in the village are grown organically. In addition, there are around 40 fish ponds for the cultivation of Gurami fish, Tilapia fish, and Patin fish. Of course, this fish farming also uses the organic concept. Initially, the organic village concept was carried out independently by the villagers. They practiced organic farming in their own homes, and provided homestays for visitors who wanted to spend the night in the village. In 2013, the village's rice products received organic certification from the Seloliman Organic Certification Institute (Lessos). Then, the village was designated as an Organic Tourism Village, and began to develop various aspects of tourism such as, coaching tour guides, promoting tourism villages, and others to date.

Accessibility

Going to Lombok Kulon Village is about 15 minutes or takes about 30 minutes from Bondowoso City. Although the road to this tourist village is quite narrow, the access road to this tourist spot is quite comfortable for vehicles to pass. Visiting Lombok Kulon Organic Tourism Village, visitors can not only enjoy culinary and organic farming.

River Tubing

Lombok Kulon tourist village is equipped with Tubing tours by utilizing the river flow in this village which is still preserved by the local community. Tubing is an activity of sliding freely on a rugged river stream using tires. Visitors can enjoy the clean and cold Wonosroyo river flow along two kilometers. There are facilities available for this river tubing activity.

Homestay

When visiting this village, Homestay is one of the most popular places for visitors. Because in this Homestay, visitors can find out in detail about the process of organic vegetable cultivation and can pick and shop directly for organic products. The facilities provided for tourists who want to spend the night are 5 homestays and the houses of local residents who are used as places to stay. Facilities provided for tourists who want to stay overnight are 5 homestays and local people's houses that are used as places to stay.
