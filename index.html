Organic Tourism Village with its Unique Organic Farms

Lombok Kulon Organic Tourism Village, located in Bondowoso Regency, East Java, is a unique destination characterized by its organic farming. In this village, almost all agricultural products are grown organically, creating an environmentally friendly and sustainable environment. Visitors can enjoy various attractions, including tubing activities, while enjoying the natural beauty and organic cultivation promoted by the local community.

Lombok Kulon Tourism Village, Natural Beauty Preserved

One of the main attractions of Lombok Kulon Organic Tourism Village is the organic fish farming around 40 ponds. Gourami, tilapia, and catfish are bred using organic concepts, showing the village's commitment to sustainable natural resource management. In addition, the village's flagship product is organic rice that has received organic certification from the Seloliman Organic Certification Institute (Lessos). The village is proudly recognized as an Organic Tourism Village, where visitors can experience firsthand the process of organic vegetable cultivation, and can even pick and shop directly for organic products.

Lombok Kulon Organic Tourism Village, a Sustainability Model for Other Villages

Lombok Kulon Organic Tourism Village is not just an ordinary tourism destination, but also an educational center for tourists and the surrounding community. The focus on developing local potential, such as organic rice, vegetables and freshwater fish, creates a tourism village concept based on the active participation of the local community. This initiative not only supports tourism, but also empowers the community to maintain environmental sustainability and develop the local economy sustainably. Lombok Kulon Organic Tourism Village serves as a model for other villages that want to combine tourism, education and sustainability in one.

Services

The following are some of the Working Group Facilities “POKJA” in Lombok Kulon Organic Tourism Village

Human Resources Working Group

Receives tourist visits and is engaged in the improvement of human resources.

Agriculture Working Group

The procedure for planting rice and vegetables using organic methods, making organic fertilizer with materials from the surrounding nature, and packaging rice from organic farming.

Fisheries Working Group

Managing fish ponds from hatchery to harvest. Provide information and training on how to grow fish using organic methods.

Attraction Working Group

Briefed the participants before the tubing started. The activity lasts for 1 hour. The guides are certified and spread out at several points to supervise, guard, and take visitor documentation.
